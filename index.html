Impact of civilizaton

Classical Greek culture, especially philosophy, had a powerful influence on ancient Rome, which carried a version of it throughout the Mediterranean and much of Europe. For this reason, Classical Greece is generally considered the cradle of Western civilization, the seminal culture from which the modern West derives many of its founding archetypes and ideas in politics, philosophy, science, and art

Art

The Greeks had elaborate art.They clearly put as much effort as they could into their temples and sculptures.Most of the art was made to honor their gods and goddesses.The most famous building and the finest buildins of the greek civilizaton was Parthenon, in Athens.Greek sculpture were usually in stone and bronze and sometimes in gold and ivory.They applied mathematical ratios to achieve aesthetic beauty. As time went by, and their skills improved still more, they sought to represent movement and emotion.

Technology

The Ancient Greeks found amazing advances in architecture and technology.Ancient Greeks invented the gear, screw, rotary mills, bronze casting techniques, water clock, water organ, torsion catapult, the use of steam to operate some experimental machines and toys, and a chart to find prime numbers.They developed surveying and mathematics to an advanced state, and many of their technical advances were published by philosophers, like Archimedes and Heron.

Literature

Some of the Greek alphabet are still used today.The Ancient Greek were very advanced in the field of Language.Greek civilizaton produced the greatest poet, Homer. Homer wrote classics like the Iliad and the Odyssey. These poems have been studied by western scholars ever since.The Greeks were the first to pioneer the art form of drama. This had its origins in the dances and songs of sacred rites, and was always associated with religious festivals. The Greeks also pioneered the writing of history as not merely the chronicling of events, but in striving for accuracy, objectivity and meaning in their accounts.

Religion

The Greeks believed in 12 major gods and goddesses.Most revered God was the chief of gods named Zeus.Different city-states were dedicated to different gods.The Olympians were worshiped the most, but there were others that received respect as well.

Warfare

Battles were fought between large formations of foot soldiers, fighting at close quarters: the majority of the casualties in a set-piece battle would obviously occur at the front of the two formations; if one of the sides turned and ran (a not infrequent occurrence) the all were in danger. Cavalry played a comparatively minor role in Greek warfare.A hoplite, or heavy-armed infantry soldier, was armed with a spear, large shield, and helmet. Better-off hoplites would have in addition a bronze breastplate and greaves. These would tend to fight in the front line, the place of most honour.Several city-sates maintained large fleets of galleys because all the city-states were situated on islands.Therefore Naval warfare was very important.
